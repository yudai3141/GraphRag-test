A rough supplement of ordinary and positive daily life, in the same first person.
This is deliberately mundane and calm — everyday routines and small good moments —
so that the memory graph gains neutral and positive material, not only trauma.

In the morning I make coffee and stand by the kitchen window. The light comes in and
the room feels quiet and warm. I like the smell of the coffee. Some mornings my husband
and I have breakfast together and talk about nothing in particular, and it feels easy.

On good days I go for a walk along the river. The water is calm and the path is familiar.
I say hello to a neighbor walking their dog. The dog is friendly and I crouch down to pet it.
The air is fresh. Walking makes my shoulders relax and my breathing slow down.

I started going to a Zumba class at the gym. At first I was unsure, but the music is fun
and the instructor is kind. Moving to the music, I forget myself for a while. Afterwards I feel
tired in a good way, and a little proud that I went. My body felt like mine again for that hour.

In the evening we cook dinner together. Chopping vegetables, the sound of the pan, the smell
of garlic. We put on music. Sometimes we just watch a comedy show and laugh at something silly.
Eating a warm meal at the table, I feel settled and safe.

I have a small garden on the balcony. I water the tomatoes and basil in the morning. Watching
them grow slowly is satisfying. It is a quiet, ordinary pleasure, nothing dramatic.

I call my friend on the phone once a week. We talk about books and small everyday things.
Her voice is comforting. After the call I feel connected and lighter.

On a calm afternoon I read on the sofa with a cup of tea. The blanket is soft. The cat sleeps
next to me. These are the moments when I feel most at ease — ordinary, safe, unremarkable.

Grocery shopping on a weekday is just grocery shopping: I pick vegetables, bread, milk, and
walk home. It is boring in a good way. Nothing happens, and that is fine.

Sometimes I accomplish a small thing at work and feel a quiet sense of competence. I finished
a report, a colleague thanked me, and I went home feeling that the day had gone well.
